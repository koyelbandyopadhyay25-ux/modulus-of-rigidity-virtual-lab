Modulus of Rigidity by Dynamic Method

Interactive virtual physics laboratory
Physics - Mechanics

OBJECTIVE

Determination of the rigidity modulus of the material of a wire by a dynamic method.

APPARATUS

Cradle with suspended wire, slide calipers, screw gauge, steel tape, weight box.

THEORY

The period T of the torsional oscillation of a body of moment of inertia I is given by,

\[ T = 2\pi\sqrt{I/C} \]

where C is the torsional couple for 1 radian twist in the suspension wire.

If the period of oscillation about the vertical axis,

  1. Of the cradle alone = T0
  2. Of the cradle and the body of known moment of inertia = T1

Then, \[ T_0 = 2\pi\sqrt{I_0/C} \quad ......... (1) \]

\[ T_1 = 2\pi\sqrt{(I_1 + I_0)/C} \quad ......... (2) \]

Where \( I_0 \), \( I_1 \) are respectively the moment of inertia of cradle, known body.

The moment of inertia \( I_1 \) of the known body (which may be a cylinder or a rectangular bar) about the vertical axis passing through its center of gravity and perpendicular to its length is calculated from the formula,

\[ I_1 = M \left(\frac{L^2}{12} + \frac{R^2}{4}\right) \text{ (for cylinder);} \quad \text{or, } I_1 = \frac{M}{12}(L^2 + b^2) \text{ (for rectangular bar)} \quad ...... (3) \]

Here M, L, b and R represent mass, length, breadth and radius of the known body respectively.

\[ \text{Now } C = \frac{\pi\eta r^4}{2l} \quad ......... (4) \]

Where \(\eta\) is the rigidity modulus of the material of the suspension wire of length l and radius r. Eliminating \( I_0 \) from Eqs (1) and (2) and using Eq (4) we get

\[ \eta = \frac{8\pi l I_1}{r^4(T_1^2 - T_0^2)} \quad ......... (5) \]

If l and r are put in meter, \( I_1 \) in kg-m2, then \(\eta\) will be in N.m-2.

PROCEDURE

  1. The mass (M) of the known body is determined by a rough balance or a spring balance while its diameter (2R) or breadth (b) and length (L) are measured by a slide callipers. Its moment of inertia (I1) is then calculated from the relation Eq (3).
  2. The cradle alone is made to perform torsional oscillations with a small angular amplitude and the time period for 30 complete oscillations is noted thrice. The mean of these three observed times when divided by 30, we get the period (T0) of the cradle.
  3. The length l of the suspension wire from its point of suspension to the point where the frame is attached, is measured by a steel tape thrice and its mean value is found out.
  4. The diameter of the suspension wire is measured by a screw gauge at least in four different places and at each place this diameter is found out in two perpendicular directions. When the mean of these diameters (d) is halved and corrected for the instrumental error of the screw gauge, we get the mean corrected radius r = (d/2) of the suspension wire.
  5. The known body is placed horizontally on the cradle and the period of oscillation (T1) of the cradle and the known body together, is similarly determined.
  6. Putting the values of I1, l, r, T1 and T0 in Eq. (5), η is calculated.

ERROR ANALYSIS

\[ \eta = \frac{8\pi l I_1}{r^4(T_1^2 - T_0^2)} = \frac{8\pi l}{r^4(T_1^2 - T_0^2)} M \left(\frac{L^2}{12} + \frac{R^2}{4}\right) \]

\[ \left(\frac{\delta \eta}{\eta}\right)_{max} = \frac{\delta l}{l} + 4\frac{\delta r}{r} + \frac{\delta (T_1^2 - T_0^2)}{(T_1^2 - T_0^2)} + \frac{\delta(L^2 + 3R^2)}{(L^2 + 3R^2)} \]

\[ = \frac{\delta l}{l} + 4\frac{\delta r}{r} + \frac{2\delta T((T_1 - T_0))}{(T_1^2 - T_0^2)} + \frac{2L\delta L + 6R\delta R}{L^2 + 3R^2} \]

Where \(\delta l\)= 2 div. of meter scale, \(\delta r\) = least count of screw gauge

\(\delta T = \delta t\), \(\delta t\)=1 smallest div. of stop watch, \(\delta L\) = v.c of slide callipers, \(\delta R\)= v.c of slide callipers

Now putting the value of l, r, T, L and R we can calculate \( \left(\frac{\delta \eta}{\eta}\right)_{max} \). Next multiplying it by 100 we get % error in '\(\eta\)'.

PRECAUTIONS:

  • The suspension wire must be perfectly straight and free from kinks.
  • The amplitude of torsional oscillation should be kept small to remain within the elastic limit.
  • The axis of oscillation must coincide with the axis of the wire.

DISCUSSIONS:

  • The dynamic method eliminates static friction issues present in static methods.
  • Care must be taken when measuring the radius of the wire since it appears in the 4th power in the formula, making it the largest source of potential error.
